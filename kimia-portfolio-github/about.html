My career so far:

From Synapses to Pixels.

I didn't start in tech; I started in the lab. With a double background in Cognitive Science and Biology, I spent my early career obsessing over how the human brain processes visual stimuli. Today, I translate those biological "bugs and features" into seamless digital experiences.

Currently, I'm at Meta Reality Labs, where I lead mixed-methods research for the next generation of AR/VR eyewear. Whether I'm using R and Python to clean biometric data or conducting longitudinal fit studies for AR hardware, my goal is the same: making technology feel like a natural extension of the human body.

Before Meta, I was at Apple and Keysight, helping Fortune 500 teams move faster by turning complex user friction into "exec-ready" strategy.
